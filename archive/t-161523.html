Firstly, it's not 4D, it's just stereoscopic 3D.
Secondly, you don't get stereoscopic 3D just by shifting your 2 scopes left and right... you need to move them in 3D before projecting to 2D, in your case you need to add a small positive/negative offset to x3. Otherwise there is no difference in horizontal shift for different depths.
And thirdly, unless you want to give people headaches, you won't make stereoscoping presets that change shape 30 times per second :rolleyes:.
