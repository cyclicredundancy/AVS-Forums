23rd August 2003, 12:02

The problem with colouring superscope models is occlusion... maximum blending monochrome objects is the cheapest way to do it.. originally the starship model from 'To Boldy Go' had blue glowy plasma on the warp nacelles and a blue deflector dish... occluding them was so damned slow and not very good though so I had to ditch them. The method I was using was to buffer restore the whole thing then render over the things in the foreground. I'm not sure that there is a better way... at least that I can understand. EL-VIS seems to have a reliable replace mode 3D going on.. but I can't make head nor tail of his code... its just too messy for me. :p

I like your idea about coders forming teams to create different parts of a preset... I did try to form a team doing something like this before.. one member working on SSC model, one on a DM etc... but it never took off.

The fractals are actually pretty simple... they are julia sets of order 2, 3, 4 and 5 morphing together with the standard tweening method of using var1*x1+var2*x2 where var1+var2=1;

I'm glad you enjoyed my presets.

The fake torus tunnel is done by intersecting a sphere and a one sheet hyperboloid to approximate (quite badly) a torus.

VJ Chmutov is something a little bit special compared to the rest of the pack, it uses a new raytracing method.
