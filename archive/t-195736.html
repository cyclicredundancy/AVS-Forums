Just use windowed mode with overlay and set desktop color then adjust your graphic card settings to send overlay to 2nd screen (very easy to do with nvidia card, just play with detonator driver settings).
So u keep avs editor on your first monitor (vga?) and a have a full screen render on second monitor(tv/projector?).

I do this at parties since years and it work pretty well.
goebish
16th December 2004, 12:29

And it's even simpler if you have a dual vga head or 2 video cards into your comp. Just set primary display (in windows xp display settings) to the screen where you want the overlay.
Move icons & task bar from the overlay screen to the other.
Don't forget to use 24/32bits display mode for the overlay to work correctly (without ugly black dots).
