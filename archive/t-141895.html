Where to start with picking apart this statement of wrongs?

Hydrogen atoms only contain two electrons when ionized and normally contain one

Because something moves fast it doesn't make it into a cloud, a cloud is a fairly densely packed volume of particles.
Electrons are not always yellowish-white they can infact vary in color as they move and change energy states when they will emit different energy photons and therefore will 'change colour' for small periods of time.

Does it matter how theoretically accurate an atom preset is? No. What matters is how good it looks.
