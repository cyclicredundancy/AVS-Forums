Use the PrintScreen button on your keyboard to take a screenshot. Or when the AVS window is active, use ALT+PrintScreen to grab that window only.

Paste it as a new image in your favourite paint program such as Photoshop or Paint Shop Pro. MS Paint will work too but it's stone-age technology compared to decent paint programs. You can get a shareware copy of Paint Shop Pro at http://www.jasc.com/.
And you can visit astalavista.com to 'register' your shareware copy.

Oh, I suppose you already know how to use PiMP or NSIS to make an installer for your presets? Make sure it auto-detects the AVS path (some people don't put it in Program Files).
