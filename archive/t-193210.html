milkdrop is done in DirectX (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DirectX), and gets your video card (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Video_card) to do alot of the hard work.

AVS (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advanced_Visualization_Studio) doesnt, it gets your cpu (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Processing_Unit) to do it all.

For something like a vis (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/VJ) application (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Application_software), the render engine (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphics_engine) is the most central part of the code; everything else is built up around it.

Milkdrop's code will be optimized (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Performance_tuning) to take advantage of the fact it has the video card always ready to lend a hand. Wheras AVS is optimised completely differently (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assembly_language).

So to answer your (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feeble-Minded) question, you (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idiot) can't do it, ever (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heat_death). There are no parts of the two programs that could just be interchanged and work like magic (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magic). There are no global format (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISO) for a 'render engine', they are all different, and everything else (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Answer_to_Life,_the_Universe,_and_Everything) is built up around them, in both cases (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suitcase).

K? (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Okay)
