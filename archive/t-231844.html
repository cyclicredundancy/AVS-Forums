...Camera?

Uh, no, I don't think we're talking about the right kind of AVS here. AVS, or "Advanced Visualization Studio", is a WinAmp plug-in that generates interesting visual displays based on the music that's currently being played in WinAmp. This has nothing at all to do with any kind of camera, webcam, or other video input. If WinAmp is giving you a "Camera not found" message for some bizarre reason, there must be some other plug-in (but not one I'm familiar with) that's causing it.

Perhaps you could try asking your question elsewhere on the WinAmp forums (http://forums.winamp.com/index.php).

(...This is assuming you're even using Winamp, and haven't been steered in the wrong direction by a Yahoo search.)

Best of luck to you.

Well im not familiar with Sy Fi's irisape, but i just downloaded and installed it for a test and found out that there should be a uninstaller on your winamp\plugins\avs folder simply called "uninst.exe" (it asks you first if you're really sure you want to uninstall the irisape), if you can't find the uninstaller then just try and locate "IrisAPE.ape" (again in your winamp\plugins\avs folder) and delete that, that should remove your problem.
