hey, mikwiththestick. Take it from me, these guys are VERY hard to impress in here. That's not bad, it means they are just drop dead f-ing primo at making presets. This is something that you need to have a love for, and keep at it. Go to deviant.art.com and look at the avs presets there; when you can get even close to those you have almost arrived. (I'm a noob myself, that's why I know this. Make like 10 or so presets that REALLY impress yourself, then make 10 or 20 more. What I'm trying to say is strive for greatness in AVS. Then with luck and the motivation to learn as you go, you may get there.)

Oh, and the best advice I can give you (for what it's worth) is try not to use so many "pre" effects such as roto-blitter and ring and dot fountain. Use Superscope, Dynamic Movement, Distance modifier. In other words, the effects that are most under your control. Yes, I'm afraid you will have to learn more than a few things. There are tutorials out there (look at the skicky threads). But it will be worth it.

Damn, Sorry for rambling on for so long!:igor:
