epileptic people-beware! :D
Not bad for a start. Set yourself little goals for what to understand and focus on understanding that one thing, and if you get stuck, just move on or ask for help in the forums. It just seems a little overwhelming if you look at lots of the more advanced superscopes, but if you try setting little goals first and concentrate on one thing, it's a lot easier to learn than if you try to learn everything at once. It's going to take a long time, and you'll always be trying to do new things, but that's what's so wonderful about AVS-it never gets old, because there's always new things to try to make :)

--Just a little friendly advice from C. Mountford about how to learn superscopes--
