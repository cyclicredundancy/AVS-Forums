I always say this but try and smooth out the transitions a bit. Even if fast jumpyness is your style you can still make things change quickly without it being instantaneous. Specifically I'm talking about your DM. An easy way to do this is to make dx and dy 'targeting' variables by doing something like this:

frame:

dx=0.9*dx+0.1*tdx;
dy=0.9*dy+0.1*tdy;

beat:

tdx=(((rand(100)-50)*.01)/1)+.03;
tdy=(((rand(100)-50)*.1)*.2)+.03;

this way dx and dy don't instantly change. To make them change faster/slower simply change the 0.9 and 0.1 to different values which are positive and add up to 1. For example dx=0.5*dx+0.5*tdx; is a much faster change and 0.98*dx+0.02*tdx; is much slower.

The other thing I would recommend is to empty the effect list out into 'main', just because it is faster and doesn't make to much of a difference to the apperance of the preset.

The only qualm that I have with hyper-fast presets is that not all music is fast and so a fast preset will look out of place when listening to chilled music. Its better to try and make your presets react so that they are faster for mor intense or loud music and slower/less intense for slow music. It can be quite hard to do it successfully but I always try to do that in my presets.

If you want to see high fps you should check out my J7 pack, it comes with a highres bonus pack, some of those presets get over 100fps in 'normal' resolutions like 320x240 on my 1GHz POS.
