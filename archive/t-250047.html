For as far as your computer is concerned, a monitor is equal to a projector. I work with projectors professionally on a daily basis.

You also speak about hardware to connect to the wall, do you mean a projection screen? If so, I suggest you only go for 'Projecta' screens. All other brands suck llama balls (in my opinion).

About the projector.. you can either use a video card with dual-monitor out, or use a VGA splitter to send the visual information to both your monitor and the projector.
I suggest you don't get a DLP projector, but one with LCD-technology; AVS has fast moving images, and DLP technology really sucks at that.

I suggest you contact a local dealer for specific details about hardware
