11th January 2003 23:34 UTC

alpha is a variable used to define the transparency of the pixels changed by the DM. Alpha ranges from 0 (no trans.) to 1 (full trans. where only the image defined in the source box is displayed). To see the effects of it in 04.avs add the line "alpha=(x+1)/2;" to the end of the pixel editbox and check the blend box.

I might have gotten the alpha values mixed up. Let me know if I did.

Ugly Edges are considered the edges in DM's that seem out of place and are side effects of some 3D DM's. In his preset if you look at the edges of the rectangle you will notice them curving and folding. If this is the effect that he is going for, then he is free to use it, but it is normally not desired. I'm not personally dissing his preset, I'm just using what is the common name given to this side effect. In order to fix it, the grid size can be increased or use some sort of tiling, etc.
