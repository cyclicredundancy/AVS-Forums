I'd have to agree with Nic01 on most of his points. (though I'd like to think I wouldn't be so blunt (no offence Nic01))

Beat responsiveness is a big thing for me. It's a big way of showing that the visuals are infact connected to the music. It doesn't necessarily have to be a big change, but change adds variety to the preset and makes it more watchable. Take a look at the attached preset (anyshape). The superscope may be nice (heck, I'm proud of it), but if it didn't rotate and change rotation on the beat it wouldn't even get a second glace. People would say "Oh, cool, a star. What other presets do I have?". I'd do it.

DMs are a good way to make your preset more interesting. They don't have to be complex, just so long as they produce a good movement. (see 01 - missle swarm for example).

Like I said in another thred, check the sticky FAQ thred if you want to learn more. Take apart other people's DMs and Superscopes. See what makes them do what they do. (I wouldn't suggest trying to take apart any of Unconed's though. It will lead to insaity.) :p
