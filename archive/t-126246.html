Well, I started to make a city with the camera moving all round and the work is going on.
I coded the buildings again from scratch and this time they are made more neat and tidy. Also, I have interchanged the y and z axes.

Previously, the y axis was the height of the building, whereas the z axis was the depth when viewed from the side if the bilding.

the initial camera position was on the side of the building.

But, now the z axis is the height of the building and x and y axes are the position of the building when viewed right from above. This make the coding a lot easier, especially when you have to position the scopes in the enviornment. you get a clearer idea when positioning on x and y axes and most of all, you dont have to bother about the z axis, as the heght of the building is fixed.
the camera has to be tuned a lot, and the buildings have to be multiplied to create a real city, as this is not a random generator.
this buildings are just placed to 2.5 of the x axis, when they go below -2.5. So, the repositioning of the buildings isnt seen.
Play with rx,ry, and rz to rotate the buildings.
t is the position on x axis. when t>2.5 it become -2.5.

What I want is rx=pi/2;rz=pi;
therefore rx=90 degrees and rz=180 degrees. this brings the building right in front of you. then I have to move the camera.
I have to experiment a more here.

A WORD OF CAUTION : Dont mess with i1 and i2, they can cause trouble, and you can end up crashing AVS. let i1=0;i2=0;cn=0; remain as it is in per frame.
