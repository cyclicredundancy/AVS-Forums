The effect lists do exactly nothing, you can move everything out of them. Remember, an effect list is only useful when you want to have a preset-inside-a-preset, but when your entire preset is in an effect list, it usually has no purpose.

Your main problem is that you just seem to have thrown a bunch of things together. Now you need to try and get the same effect (or something close to it), but by only keeping the effects that actually do something. Most components have virtually no effect on the end-result in your preset (e.g. the Trans / Bump).

Don't expect to be come an AVS expert in one day... but if you stick to it and keep trying out new things, you'll figure it out.
