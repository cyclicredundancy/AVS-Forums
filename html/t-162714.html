Optimizing tips
This is getting better. But why did you remove "Cubex - Anarchy Smoke Fire"? It was one of the best presets :)
You still have a lot to learn, ripping is normal for beginners, it's part of learning.
Here are some speed-up tips:
- Instead of using 4 fadeouts with low velocity, use one with higher velocity.

- Set lower grid size in simple DMs, for example if you're uning DM for simple shifting or zooming, why calculate same thing 16*16=256 times?

- In DMs/SuperScopes don't calculate on Point/Pixel things that don't change. For example: You have SSC and per-point you have "r=i*2*$PI". But "$PI*2" never changes, you can calculate in on Inizialization of the preset. Or if you have variable "t" in SuperScope, every frame you add some number to it: "t=t+0.05" and on point you use the sine of it: "x=sin(t)". For example you have 800 points. So your CPU does 800 same calculations every frame! Calsulate sine of "t" every frame instead: "t=t+.05; st=sin(t);" and on point use that value: "x=st;".

- Try making your own movements, like this:

r=r+sin(d*$PI*5);
...instead of 10 "swirling both ways at once" movements.

- Use faster APEs insted of slower analogs. For example: Convolution instead of Blur, Multiplier instead of (Fast-) Brightness and ColorMap instead of Unique Tone.

- Don't use THAT many effectlists, they don't realy do anything most of the time. I attached an example how it is possabile to optimize the preset by removing 90% of effectlists. Isn't it easier to edit like this?
