I'm a bit confused, your post wasn't all too clear to me...

You're talking about 'downloaded plug-ins' inside the AVS preset list.
Plugins do not show up in that list: Only the directories in the /avs/ dir and .avs files are displayed.
Don't you mean preset packs? AVS 'plugins' are called presets, (as AVS is a plugin itself). Please refer to them correctly to prevent confusion.

Switching between presets goes the easiest by rightclicking somewhere in the AVS window, then selecting a directory > preset.

That little round button, which brings up a popup menu of visualization functions, is meant to be accessed with the left button. I've never tried using the right button on it, but it's logical to assume that it might bring up a different menu, one that doesn't list the hundreds of installed presets.

Is that even AVS in your screenshot? For some reason it looks more like Milkdrop to me. Although rather similar to AVS in many ways, that's a completely different visualization plugin, one of three included with WinAmp. You can use your preferences to confirm whether or not AVS is the currently selected visualization plugin.
