3D water effect as in the Water thing done in 3D, or looking at a picture through water? The latter can be done with Water Bump quite easily, but a 3D water effect would be the same as a 3D blitter feedback or blur or anything else (that is, if you had the water you could get the rest of it, and vice versa). The problem with doing true 3D trans is this: You're changing the complexity from n to n*sqrt(n), that is, n^2 to n^3. Not only that, but you now need to a) layer each successive z-plane (uber, uber, uber, uber, uber slow, and static, too), or b) have a moving camera that raytraces the entire scene w/transparency. If you want to know how slow THAT is, get a decent raytracing application (POV-ray or such), then find a scene with a whole shitload of smoke effects. It'll take about five minutes to produce one scene at maybe 400x300 at best.
So all in all a 3D effect would have to be very low-framerate and very low-rez, until everyone has dual 10Ghz processors in their system :)
