Final Whack isn't a plugin, it's a pack of presets for the AVS plug-in

When I said clicking I meant the small visualizer in the small window of the main window (not the video/vis dropdown)


To enable avs, this is what you should do:
go to: options>preferences>plugins>visualization

select Advanced Visualization Studio v2.81b [vis_avs.dll]

hit [Ctrl]+[shift]+[k]

AVS should open now, and when you right-click the avs window, final whack should be in that list

9th November 2005 21:43 UTC

Try setting both plugin directories to "...\winamp\plugins", since that is where the actual plugin DLL files (for visualizations and practically everything else) are located.

These plugins automatically look in the "avs" and other subdirectories (depending on the plugin) for their components and presets and other data, so those locations shouldn't need to be configured. ("Presets" meaning the actual AVS files included in visualization packs such as "Final Whack". The visualization itself is AVS.)
