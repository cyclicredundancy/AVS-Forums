It's either alt+printscreen, shift+printscreen, or ctrl+printscreen. I just do all three at the right time, since I can't remember which it is.

When AVS is windowed, if you take a screenshot (when the AVS window is selected) you only get the AVS window and whatever's in it. If AVS isn't selected, you get the entire screen (or what the other program that's selected does). When AVS is in fullscreen you get the screenshot, but no window.
